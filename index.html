Operators

In math, an operator is a symbol that tells us what kind of math operation to perform. The four basic operators are:

Addition (+): This operator tells us to add two numbers together. For example, (3 + 2 = 5).

Subtraction (-): This operator tells us to subtract one number from another. For example, (5 - 2 = 3).

Division (÷): This operator tells us to divide one number by another. For example, (8 ÷ 2 = 4).

Multiplication (×): This operator tells us to multiply two numbers. For example, (4 × 3 = 12).

These operators help us solve math problems by showing us what to do with the numbers.
